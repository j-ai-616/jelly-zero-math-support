How to Play

The goal is simple: use the given numbers and math buttons to create an equation whose final result is exactly 0.

Try different combinations, check your result, and discover jelly reactions as you solve puzzles.

Strategy Tips

Jelly Zero Math becomes more interesting when you do not simply multiply by 0. Try to train your brain by combining numbers, changing signs, using brackets, and applying square roots.

For example, if the numbers are 0, 1, 5, 2, you can use Combine to make 10:

10 − (5 × 2) = 0

This means the puzzle is not only about finding the fastest answer. It is also about discovering creative ways to build zero.

Tip: Look carefully at the screenshots above to see how number buttons, operators, brackets, square roots, and Combine can work together.

Puzzle Philosophy

Jelly Zero Math is designed as a light brain-refresh puzzle game. You can enjoy it during small breaks, while still giving your brain a creative math workout.

Some puzzles may feel tricky, and not every path is obvious right away. If a puzzle feels too difficult, feel free to move on to a new one.

Future updates may refine puzzle generation and add more playful challenges.

Button Guide

Numbers

Number Buttons

Tap the given number buttons to add them to your equation. Each puzzle gives you a limited set of numbers, so the challenge is to use them creatively.

Operators

+, −, ×, ÷

Use basic arithmetic operators to connect numbers and build an equation that results in zero.

Brackets

( )

Brackets change the calculation order and help you control which part of the equation is solved first.

Square Root

√

The square root button applies a square root to a number or expression. For example, √9 becomes 3.

Negative

(-)

The (-) button changes a number into a negative value. This is different from the subtraction operator.

Combine

Combine

Combine joins selected adjacent digits into a larger number when the puzzle allows it. For example, 1 and 2 can become 12.

Check

Check

Tap Check when you think your equation equals zero. The app will evaluate your equation and show whether your answer is correct.

New Puzzle

New Puzzle

Tap New Puzzle to start a new challenge with a different set of numbers.
